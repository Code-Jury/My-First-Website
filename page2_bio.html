BIOLOGY

BIOLOGY Biology is derived from two Greek words, "bio" meaning "life" and "logy" meaning "studying of life." Biology is then defined as the scientific study of life, that is, the study of plants and animals. There are two major branches of Biology which are: 1. Botany: The study of plants. 2. Zoology: The study of animals. Other branches of Biology are: 1. Microbiology 2. Biochemistry 3. Biomedical science 4. Physiology 5. Marine biology 6. Genetics 7. Ecology 8. Anatomy 9. Biophysics 10. Biotechnology 11. Cell Biology 12. Immunology etc. SCIENTIFIC PROCESSES 1. OBSERVATION: As a student of biology, you must learn to observe changes around you. 2. HYPOTHESIS: Then, you can give a tentative statement explaining your observations. For instance, you can say that air makes an object light after observing a balloon float on water. 3. EXPERIMENTS: As Galileo Galilei has shown, we don't speak facts. We show facts. Now, you get two objects and fill one with air and the other with water. The object filled with water is called the control group. It's meant to counter the hypothesis. 4. THEORY: So at the end of the day, you conclude that air makes an object float after your experiments. Your conclusion is called a theory. A theory is an acceptable statement for an action after series of successful experiments. 5. LAW: When a theory is applied in wide areas, it will become a law. We cannot actually call your theory of floatation a law because there's a law of floatation by Archimedes that gives a better reason why things float.

CLASSIFICATION OF LIVING THINGS

VIRUSES:

They are very small that they can only be seen through the more powerful electron microscope. They behave like non-living things (crystals) outside the cell and living things within the cell. They are the major causes of the deadliest plant and animal diseases.
KINGDOM MONERA: They include the simplest living organisms: bacteria and more ancient blue-green algae. They have prokaryotic cells (cells without a defined nuclues). BACTERIA can be seen with a light microscope. They occur singly, in groups or in chains. They produce energy from simple inorganic compounds and reproduce very fast through binary fission (A parent cell divides into binary or two daughter cells). Some motile bacteria possess a flagellum (a whip-like structure). BLUE-GREEN ALGA also called CYANOBACTERIA contain chlorophyll and can produce their own food. They are found mainly in freshwater and in damp places on land. So, you might have come across them. They exist as single cells or as colonies.
