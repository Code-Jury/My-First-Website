GOVERNMENT POWER AND AUTHORITY POWER Power is the ability to control the actions of others through the means of sanctions. TYPES OF POWER POLITICAL: Possessed by the government. MILITARY: Possessed by the armed forces. PHYSICAL : Use of force. ECONOMIC : Use of resources. SOURCES OF POWER The constitution By inheritance Position of Authority Through coercion The use of charisma AUTHORITY Authority is the power or right to give orders and enforce obedience. SOURCES OF AUTHORITY TRADITIONAL AUTHORITY : Based on existing customs and traditions. LEGAL AUTHORITY: Derived from a body of rules and regulations governing a society. CHARISMATIC AUTHORITY: Derived from one's exceptional personal characteristics. TYPES OF AUTHORITY Political Authority Coercive Authority Delegate Authority Traditional Authority Legal Authority DIFFERENCES BETWEEN POWER AND AUTHORITY Authority can be exercised without the use of force while power must involve the use of force and sanctions. Also, authority is known to be legitimate while power may be illegitimate e.g military power. And authority can be delegated but power cannot be. @Andrew Newman
