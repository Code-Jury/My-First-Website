COMMERCE

Commerce is all activities that are involved in the distribution and exchange of goods and services. The three major activities are production, consumption and exchange and the central focus of commerce is exchange. SCOPE OF COMMERCE This covers all the activities that involve the distribution and exchange of goods and services for the satisfaction of the people. These activities are: 1. Trading 2. Warehousing 3. Insurance 4. Communication 5. Transportation 6. Banking 7. Advertising 8. Tourism DIVISONS OF COMMERCE Commerce is divided into two: 1. Trade 2. Aids to Trade TRADE Trade is the act of buying and selling of goods and services. TYPES OF TRADE 1. HOME TRADE: Buying and selling of goods and services within a geographical area. Also called internal/domestic trade. It is further divided into A. WHOLESALING: Buying in bulk and selling in small quantities to the retailers. B. RETAILING: Buying in small quantities and selling to the final consumers. 2. FOREIGN TRADE: Buying and selling beyond the geographical area of a country. It involves two or more counties. Also called international trade. It is further divided into A. EXPORT: Sale of goods and services to other countries. Examples are selling petroleum and opening a bank branch in a foreign country. B. IMPORT: Buying of goods and services from other countries. Examples are importing vehicles and technical services. C. ENTREPORT: Also called re-export trade. Importing goods and services and re-exporting them to other nations. An example is someone from Benin buying yams from Nigeria and reselling them to Germany. AIDS TO TRADE 1. BANKING: It makes trading possible by providing funds in form of loans and overdrafts. 2. TRANSPORTATION: It carries raw materials, goods and people from one place to another. 3. ADVERTISING: Creating awareness about the existence of a product. 4. WAREHOUSING: Storing goods until when they are needed. 5. COMMUNICATION: Sending and receiving information from one place to another. 6. INSURANCE: A security made against loss. It is meant to protect businesses and individuals against loss or liabilities. 7. TOURISM: It facilitates trade by increasing trading activities at tourist centres. FUNCTIONS OF COMMERCE 1. Mass production of goods and services 2. Raising of capital 3. Employment opportunities 4. Transport 5. Warehousing functions 6. Unity among nations 7. Improvement in the standard of living 8.
